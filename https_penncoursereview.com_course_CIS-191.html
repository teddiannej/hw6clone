CIS 191

Linux/Unix Skills

Unix, in its many forms, runs much of the world's computer infrastructure, from cable modems and cell phones to the giant clusters that power Google and Amazon. This half-credit course provides a thorough introduction to Unix and Linux. Topics will range from critical basic skills such as examin and editing files, compiling programs and writing shell scripts, to higher level topics such as the architecture of Unix and its programming model. The material learned is applicable to many classes, including CIS 240, CIS 331, CIS 341, CIS 371, and CIS 380.

This course is a .5 credit course that in an overview of the Linux and Unix programming language. Students had high remarks for the course, one stating that “skills I learned were very spread out over different topics and pretty practical and useful.”

Although this class is stated to be taught by Boon Thau Loo, it was actually taught by the TA’s Seth Shannin and Kevin Xu who received high remarks like “Support from the teaching staff has been great” and “Seth and Kevin were shining stars as instructors.”

This class is intended for sophomores in the computer science department. However, any student trying to learn Unix and Linux programming languages. Workload was stated to be “manageable” and “actually appropriate for .5 credit course.” Homework was said to be easy at the beginning but ramps up with more difficult projects towards the end.

This half credit course provides a thorough introduction to Unix and Linux. The course explores skills such as examining and editing files, compiling programs, writing shell scripts, and also discusses the architecture of Unix and its programming model.

Students generally found this class to be “very interesting and helpful,” and decided that the material was placed well into the “broader context” of its applications. Students indicated that the logistics of this course could be improved, and commented frequently on the decrease in content and homework as the semester passed. Overall, they considered the course to be “exceptional” nonetheless.

Perry Metzger was overall judged to be an “excellent” instructor. Many students complimented his “expertise in Linux,” his passion for the material, and his “informative and amusing” lectures. Students were especially impressed with his presentation of material given that this was his first time teaching, and expect that his work will “improve dramatically” in the future. Students suggested that Metzger fix some logistical issues, such as running the class over its allotted time and posting homeworks more consistently, but seemed overwhelmingly forgiving of these “kinks” in his first year.

Students felt that this class required “too much work” relative to its half-credit status, and also disliked the regular quizzes at the beginning of each class. However, students seemed passionate enough about learning this material that many complaints regarding homework actually suggested having more near the end of the semester.
