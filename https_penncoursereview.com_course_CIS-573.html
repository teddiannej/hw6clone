CIS 573

Software Engineering

Writing a "program" is easy. Developing a "software product", however, introduces numerous challenges that make it a much more difficult task. This course will look at how professional software engineers address those challenges, by investigating best practices from industry and emerging trends in software engineering research. Topics will focus on software maintenance issues, including: test case generation and test suite adequacy; code analysis verification and model checking; debugging and fault localization; refactoring and regression testing; and software design and quality.

Writing a "program" is easy. Developing a "software product", however,
introduces numerous challenges that make it a much more difficult task. This course
looks at how professional software engineers address those challenges, by investigating
best practices from industry and emerging trends in software engineering research.
Topics focus on software maintenance issues, including: test case generation and test
suite adequacy; code analysis verification and model checking; debugging and fault
localization; refactoring and regression testing; and software design and quality.
Many students thought that the class was “relevant [to] work after graduation.”
One student noted that the course may be “one of the most useful courses in [terms of
searching] for jobs.” However, a good amount of other students felt that many of the
subjects taught were “not of any practical [value].” Specifically, they complained that the
assignments involved too much theory, and did not contain much “programming
content.” A student suggested that a final project would have been a good way to apply a
“software development process” in the class.
Several students praised Professor Murphy for his organization, saying that he
“did a really good job of designing the [brand new] course.” One student who did not
enjoy the course material remarked that Professor Murphy’s “teaching made it
interesting.”
A few students thought that “excessive class time [was] spent copying notes”
instead of paying attention to the lectures. A majority of the class did not feel that the
assignments were too much work, and some even found the course to be “too easy for
CIS students.” Students who found the course to be practical on the job strongly
recommended it to students within the major.
