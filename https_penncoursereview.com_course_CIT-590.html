CIT 590

Prog Lang And Techniques

Introduction to fundamental concepts of programming and computer science for students who have little or no experience in these areas. Principles of modern object-oriented programming languages: abstraction, types, polymorphism, encapsulation, and inheritance. Basic algorithmic techniques and informal complexity analysis. Graphical user interfaces. Substantial programming assignments in Python and Java. This course is for students who do not have an academic background in computer science and who are not pursuing the Master's in Computer Information Technology and who are not graduate students in the CIS Department.

This course is an introduction to fundamental concepts of programming and computer science for students who have little or no experience in these areas. Principles of modern object-oriented programming languages: abstraction, types, polymorphism, encapsulation, and inheritance will be covered. Other topics include basic algorithmic techniques and informal complexity analysis, graphical user interfaces and substantial programming assignments in Java.

Overall, both the lectures and labs of this course were described as being informative and especially helpful for programming beginners. Some indicated that the content was very practical and hoped that the course could be extended to explore Java programming more in-depth.

Most students did not directly comment on Professor Phillips, yet all indicated that they had learned a lot from the course. Some noted the large amount of work that was assigned.

Due to the number of students in the class who were in fact well-versed in programming, some claimed “the competition [was] sky high,” and the grading was harsh. This course is intended for graduate students who are not in the Master's in Computer Information Technology program, or the CIS Department. Other graduate students, especially those in SEAS programs, will find this course especially helpful.
